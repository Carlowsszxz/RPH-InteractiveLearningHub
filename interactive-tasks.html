Study Tips — Philippine History

Practical steps to read sources, compare accounts, and think historically

Contextualize Sources

Ask: who wrote this, when, and why? Use that to weigh reliability and perspective.

Contextualize each source: authorship, date, purpose, audience.

Cross-check & Visualize

Place events on maps or timelines and compare sources to reveal patterns and contradictions.

Compare accounts with maps, timelines, and other sources to spot differences.

Explain & Argue

Summarize your view in 2–3 sentences and support it with specific evidence from sources.

Discuss interpretations with peers; write a short evidence-based response.
